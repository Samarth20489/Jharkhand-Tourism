About Jharkhand

The Land of Forests 🌿

Jharkhand, carved out of the southern part of Bihar on November 15, 2000, is a treasure trove of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The name "Jharkhand" literally translates to "The Land of Forests" — and the state justifies this name with its vast expanses of dense forests, rolling hills, cascading waterfalls, and vibrant wildlife.

Located in eastern India, Jharkhand shares its borders with Bihar, West Bengal, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and Uttar Pradesh. The state capital, Ranchi, sits atop the Chotanagpur Plateau, providing a pleasant climate and scenic landscapes throughout the year.

🏔️

Geography

Spread across 79,714 sq km on the Chotanagpur Plateau, featuring hills, valleys, rivers and dense forests.

🎭

Tribal Culture

Home to 32 tribal communities including Santhals, Mundas, Oraons and Ho, with rich art, music and dance traditions.

🛕

Spiritual Heritage

Features Baidyanath Jyotirlinga, Rajrappa Temple, and sacred Parasnath Hill — a Jain pilgrimage center.

🌊

Waterfalls

Blessed with stunning waterfalls — Hundru, Jonha, Dassam, and Lodh Falls, the highest waterfall in the state.

🐘

Wildlife

Betla & Hazaribagh National Parks shelter elephants, tigers, leopards, sambhar deer and over 170 bird species.

☀️

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers the most pleasant weather. Monsoon (Jul–Sep) transforms waterfalls into spectacular sights.

Rich Cultural Tapestry

Jharkhand's tribal communities celebrate unique festivals like Sarhul (spring festival), Karma (harvest), Tusu and Sohrai (cattle festival). Their art forms — Paitkar paintings, Sohrai art, and Khovar art — are recognized worldwide for their intricate beauty.

The state is also known for its mineral wealth, being one of the top producers of coal, iron ore, copper, mica, and uranium in India. This has earned it the title "The Ruhr of India".

How to Reach Jharkhand

By Air: Birsa Munda Airport, Ranchi is connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata and Bangalore.

By Rail: Ranchi, Dhanbad, Jamshedpur and Bokaro are well-connected railway junctions on the South Eastern Railway network.

By Road: NH-33, NH-2, and NH-75 connect Jharkhand to neighboring states. State transport and private buses operate regularly.
