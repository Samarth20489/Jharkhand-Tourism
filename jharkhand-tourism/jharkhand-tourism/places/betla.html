About Betla National Park

Betla National Park, located in the Palamau district of Jharkhand, is one of the first national parks in India to be included under Project Tiger. Spread over 1,135 sq km of the Palamau Tiger Reserve, the park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

The park is home to a diverse range of fauna including Royal Bengal Tigers, wild elephants, leopards, sambhar deer, nilgai, wild boar, and over 170 species of birds. The dense sal and bamboo forests create a breathtaking landscape that changes dramatically with the seasons.

The historic Palamau Fort ruins, located inside the park, add an element of historical intrigue. Visitors can enjoy jeep safaris, elephant rides, and nature walks. The park also has several watchtowers for wildlife spotting.
