About Parasnath Hill

Parasnath Hill, also known as Shikharji or Sammet Shikhar, is the highest point in Jharkhand at 1,365 meters (4,478 feet) above sea level. Located in the Giridih district, it is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites for the Jain community, as 20 out of 24 Jain Tirthankaras are believed to have attained Moksha (liberation) here.

The hill is topped with a cluster of white Jain temples that gleam beautifully against the sky. Pilgrims undertake a challenging 27-km trek (round trip) from the base town of Madhuban to reach the summit temples. The trek passes through dense forests rich in biodiversity, with various species of trees, birds, and butterflies.

The panoramic views from the summit are absolutely stunning — on clear days, you can see the vast plains of Jharkhand stretching to the horizon. The hill is also a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts and nature lovers. The best time to visit is during the cooler months when the trek is most comfortable.
