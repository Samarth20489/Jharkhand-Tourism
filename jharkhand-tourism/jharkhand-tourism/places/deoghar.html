About Baidyanath Dham

Deoghar, literally meaning "Abode of the Gods," is home to Baba Baidyanath Dham — one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. This ancient temple complex is one of the most important pilgrimage centers in India, drawing millions of devotees annually.

The main temple complex houses 22 temples, with the central shrine dedicated to Baba Baidyanath (Lord Shiva). During the holy month of Shravan (July-August), the town witnesses the grand Shravan Mela where millions of Kanwarias carry sacred Ganges water from Sultanganj to offer at the Baidyanath temple — making it one of the largest religious gatherings in the world.

Beyond the temple, Deoghar offers several attractions including Nandan Pahar (an amusement park on a hill), Trikut Pahar (a three-peaked hill with ropeway), Satsang Ashram, and the beautiful Naulakha Mandir built in the architectural style of the famous Belur Math.
