About Netarhat

Netarhat, fondly called the "Queen of Chotanagpur," is a pristine hill station nestled at an altitude of 3,700 feet in the Latehar district of Jharkhand. It is one of the few untouched hill stations left in India, offering breathtaking views of sunrise and sunset over the vast expanse of forests and valleys.

The Sunrise Point at Netarhat is the main attraction, where visitors gather in the early morning hours to witness a spectacular display of colors as the sun rises over the misty hills. The Magnolia Point, named after the magnolia trees in the area, offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and is a popular sunset viewpoint.

The region is covered with dense forests of sal, pine, and rhododendron, and the cool climate makes it a perfect retreat from the summer heat. Netarhat is also home to the prestigious Netarhat Vidyalaya, one of India's finest residential schools. Nearby attractions include Upper and Lower Ghagri Falls, Koel View Point, and Lodge Falls.
